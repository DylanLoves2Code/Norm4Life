The Moth Joke

Conan O'Brien · Late Night · 2017 · 7 minutes, 4 seconds

On the Subject of the Moth Joke

In 2017, Norm Macdonald sat down on Conan O'Brien's program and told a joke that takes approximately seven minutes to deliver. The joke is about a moth. The punchline is not about a moth.

The structure is a Norm Macdonald original: a shaggy-dog story in the tradition of the form, wherein the length of the telling becomes the point of the telling. He is not buying time. He is spending it, deliberately, with complete composure, knowing exactly what he is doing.

The audience begins laughing about forty seconds in. Not because anything funny has been said, but because they have already understood that something funny is being done to them. This is the distinction Norm Macdonald made his career out of.

The punchline, when it arrives, is both earned and unearned. It is a real joke. It is also not a joke at all. He delivers it as though reading a sentence from a manual. Conan laughs for a long time. So does everyone else. Then Norm sits very still, satisfied.

Transcript — The Moth Joke
A moth goes into a podiatrist's office. The podiatrist says, "What seems to be the problem?" The moth says, "Doc, I don't know where to begin. I go to work for Gregory Illinivich and all day long I work. And for what? For a few lousy dollars. And meanwhile Gregory sits at home, does nothing, makes more money than me. I got a wife, doc. She's a mess. She's a drunk. She runs around on me. I got a son. He's turning out bad. He don't listen to me. He's got no respect. You think that's easy to take? I come home one day, my mother's sick. She's living with us, she's driving me crazy. And my brother, he's in trouble again. I go to the bar for a drink. I can't even afford a drink. My whole life, doc, is just — it's darkness and it's going nowhere." The podiatrist says, "Well, it sounds like you need a psychiatrist. Why did you come to me?" And the moth says, "The light was on."
